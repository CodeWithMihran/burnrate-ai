# ECONOMICS

If Credex deployed this tool tomorrow, the first question is what a converted lead is worth. Assume the tool surfaces teams that can meaningfully reduce AI spend through credits-backed procurement or better vendor mix. If an average converted customer ends up moving $1,500/month of AI spend through Credex and Credex captures a conservative 15% gross margin on that volume, then monthly gross profit per converted customer is about $225. Annualized, that is roughly $2,700 in gross profit before retention effects. If the average customer stays for 18 months, the gross-profit LTV is about $4,050.

Now look at CAC by channel. Cold founder outreach is labor-heavy but cash-light. If one operator can send 80 targeted messages in a day and book 4 serious tool users, the cash CAC is near zero and the real CAC is time. Community posting and teardown threads are similar: low cash cost, moderate time cost, and uneven output. The highest-leverage channel is likely earned distribution via “share your audit” loops and founder screenshots, because the tool itself becomes content. I would model early CAC like this: outbound-assisted lead capture at $20-40 implied CAC, community-led discovery at $10-25 implied CAC, and product-led shares below $10 implied CAC once the loop starts working.

Profitability depends on conversion from audit -> consultation -> credit purchase. Using the $4,050 gross-profit LTV estimate, even a $100 CAC would be attractive. Suppose 100 completed audits produce 25 captured emails, 8 qualified consultations, and 2 paying customers. That is a 2% audit-to-paid conversion rate and a 25% consultation-to-paid conversion rate. At that level, the tool is already plausible as an efficient acquisition asset because the marginal cost of an audit is low.

To reach $1M ARR-equivalent contribution in 18 months, Credex would need roughly $83K in monthly recurring gross profit impact from this channel. Using the same $225 monthly gross profit per customer, that means around 370 active converted customers attributable to the tool. Backing into the funnel, if audit-to-paid lands at 2%, Credex would need about 18,500 completed audits over that period, or roughly 1,000 audits per month by the later stages. That is ambitious, but not absurd if distribution, sharing, and founder-focused positioning all work together.
