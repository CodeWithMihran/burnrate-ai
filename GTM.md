# GTM

The exact target user is not “startups” in the abstract. It is a founder, engineering manager, or technical chief of staff at a seed to Series A software company with 5 to 40 employees, using multiple AI subscriptions informally across the team, but without a procurement owner or finance analyst doing vendor optimization. This is the team that is large enough to leak money, but still scrappy enough that nobody has time to build an internal spend dashboard.

Right before they want this tool, they are usually in one of three moments: reviewing SaaS spend, reacting to a credit-card bill that feels higher than expected, or noticing that several people on the team are all paying for overlapping AI tools. What they search is very practical and cost-oriented: “ChatGPT Team vs Enterprise pricing,” “Cursor vs Copilot for teams,” “Claude Team pricing,” “best AI coding tool for startup team,” or “how much are startups spending on AI tools.” On social channels, the pre-intent version is often a founder complaining about AI burn in public, not someone saying “I need an audit tool.”

The first 100 users with zero paid budget would come from channel-specific execution, not generic posting. First, I would cold DM founders and eng managers who are already publicly talking about Cursor, Copilot, Claude, or “AI stack” decisions on X. The ask would not be “try my startup,” but “I built a free AI spend audit; want me to run your stack through it and send the result?” Second, I would post teardown-style content in Indie Hackers, relevant startup Slack communities, and founder-heavy subreddits, using concrete examples like “3-person team paying for ChatGPT Business when Plus would cover 90% of usage.” Third, I would publish a founder-facing comparison post built from the product itself: “What AI teams overspend on first.”

The unfair distribution angle for Credex is that this is not only a content or utility play. Credex can actually monetize the discovered savings path. Most calculators stop at “you could save money.” Credex can follow that with “we can help you buy the replacement credits cheaper.” That makes the audit both a lead magnet and a pre-qualification mechanism.

Week-one traction, if the tool works, would look like 100 completed audits, 20-30 report captures, 5-10 high-savings cases above $500/month, and at least 3 booked consultations. Those are small numbers in consumer terms, but strong numbers for an early B2B lead-gen wedge.
