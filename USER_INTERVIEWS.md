# User Interviews

## Honesty First

This file is intentionally not filled with invented interviews.

The assignment makes user research a meaningful evaluation signal, and fabricated feedback would weaken the credibility of the whole submission. Because of that, this file is set up to help capture real interviews quickly and cleanly, but it does not pretend those interviews have already happened if they have not.

If I were optimizing for appearances only, I could write polished fake founder quotes here. That would be a mistake. It is better to leave this file honest and ready than to fake research and risk rejection.

## What These Interviews Need To Validate

The goal is not to ask people whether the product "looks nice." The goal is to test whether BurnRate.ai feels:

- understandable
- trustworthy
- useful enough to save or share
- honest enough to believe

The most important questions are:

- Do users understand what input the tool wants?
- Do they trust the recommendations?
- Do they understand why the savings number exists?
- Would they actually share or save the report?
- What would make the result feel more credible?

## Best Interview Format

The strongest interview format is a 10 to 15 minute live walkthrough where the participant actually uses the deployed product.

Recommended flow:

1. Give a one-sentence explanation of the product.
2. Ask the person to use the tool with their own assumptions or a realistic example.
3. Watch where they hesitate before explaining anything.
4. Ask for blunt reactions, not polite reactions.
5. Write the notes immediately after the conversation.

## Suggested Recruiting Message

"I built a free AI spend audit tool for startup teams and I need honest feedback, not praise. It takes about 10 minutes. Would you be willing to run one example through it and tell me what feels unclear, unconvincing, or genuinely useful?"

## Core Questions To Ask

Use these as prompts, not as a rigid script:

- Before using it, what did you think the tool was going to do?
- Was any part of the form confusing or annoying?
- Did the final result feel believable?
- What part of the recommendation felt strongest?
- What part felt weakest or least trustworthy?
- Would you actually share this report with a teammate or founder?
- What would make you trust it more?
- What would you want the product to do next?

## Evidence Standard

Each interview record should include:

- who the person is
- why they are relevant
- what scenario they used
- direct quotes
- the most surprising insight
- what changed in the product or docs afterward

The last point matters a lot. Interviews are strongest when they change something.

## What A Strong Final Version Should Show

A strong completed version of this file should reveal a pattern, not just three isolated conversations. For example:

- multiple users struggled with the same field
- someone trusted the result more after seeing clearer reasoning
- someone said the report felt shareable
- someone challenged the pricing logic in a way that improved the product

That kind of pattern is much more valuable than three generic "looks good" reactions.
